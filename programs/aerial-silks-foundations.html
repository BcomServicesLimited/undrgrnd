Learn Aerial Silks on the Gold Coast — Beginner Classes at UNDRGRND Movement Southport

Aerial silks is one of the most visually striking and physically rewarding disciplines in the world of aerial arts. Performed on two lengths of suspended fabric, aerial silks combines strength, flexibility, grace, and artistic expression into a single practice that challenges and transforms the body in ways that conventional fitness cannot. At UNDRGRND Movement in Southport, our beginner aerial silks classes are designed to make this extraordinary discipline accessible to Gold Coast adults of all fitness levels — no experience, strength, or flexibility required to begin.

What Is Aerial Silks?

Aerial silks — also known as aerial fabric or tissu — is a circus and aerial arts discipline in which performers climb, wrap, and move through sequences using two lengths of fabric hung from a rigging point. Unlike trapeze or aerial hoop, silks offer a uniquely tactile and expressive medium: the fabric wraps around the body to create holds, poses, and transitions that are both functional and beautiful.

On the Gold Coast, aerial silks has grown steadily in popularity as adults seek fitness experiences that are more engaging, creative, and community-oriented than traditional gym workouts. UNDRGRND Movement's Southport studio offers one of the Gold Coast's most welcoming and safety-focused environments for learning aerial silks from the ground up.

Benefits of Aerial Silks Training

The physical benefits of regular aerial silks practice are extensive. Full-body strength is the most immediate and noticeable result — particularly in the upper body, core, and grip. Climbing and holding positions on the silks requires and develops muscular endurance that is difficult to replicate through conventional exercise. Gold Coast students who train consistently report significant improvements in pull-up strength, core stability, and overall body composition within weeks of beginning.

Flexibility improves naturally through aerial silks practice. The positions and transitions required in silks work progressively stretch the hips, hamstrings, shoulders, and spine — areas that are commonly tight in adults who spend long hours sitting. Unlike static stretching, the flexibility gains from aerial silks are functional and dynamic, meaning they translate directly into improved everyday movement and reduced injury risk.

Grace and body awareness develop through the practice of moving intentionally in three dimensions. Aerial silks teaches students to understand their body in space — to feel where their limbs are, to control their movement, and to express themselves through physical form. This heightened body awareness has benefits that extend far beyond the studio, improving posture, coordination, and physical confidence in everyday life.

Safety is the foundation of everything we do at UNDRGRND Movement's Southport studio. For Gold Coast adults who are new to aerial arts, the structured safety approach at our Southport studio means that the learning environment is never intimidating or rushed. Every student progresses at their own pace, and no one is pushed to attempt a skill before they have the technique and strength to do so safely. Overcoming Fear and Building Trust in Your Body

One of the most profound aspects of aerial silks training is the process of overcoming fear. For many Gold Coast students, the first few classes involve confronting a fear of heights, a lack of trust in their own strength, or a deep-seated belief that they are not the kind of person who can do something like this. Watching those beliefs dissolve over the course of a few sessions is one of the most rewarding things our Southport instructors witness.

The process of learning to trust the silks — and your own body — is transformative. When you discover that you can support your own weight, that you can climb, that you can hold a position you never thought possible, it changes the way you see yourself. This is the deeper value of aerial silks training, and it is something that Gold Coast students at UNDRGRND Movement experience regularly.

Why Aerial Silks Is Perfect for Beginners

Aerial silks is uniquely well-suited to beginners because the fabric itself provides support and security that other aerial disciplines do not. Unlike aerial hoop or trapeze, where the apparatus is rigid, silks can be wrapped around the body to create holds that feel secure and controlled. This means that beginners can experience the sensation of being suspended and supported from their very first class, without needing significant strength or experience.

At our Southport studio, the beginner aerial silks curriculum is carefully sequenced to build confidence and competence progressively. Students start with footlocks and ground-level wraps, then progress to low-height climbs, then to basic poses and transitions. Each step builds on the last, and no student moves forward until they are ready. Gold Coast adults who have tried aerial silks at UNDRGRND Movement consistently describe the experience as more accessible than they expected.
